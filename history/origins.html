The Origins of Voith

The Founding of Voith

In 1867, Johann Matthäus Voith established a small mechanical workshop in Heidenheim, Germany. He focused on improving industrial machinery, particularly for the paper and hydropower industries. His innovations quickly gained recognition, laying the foundation for Voith’s future success as a leader in engineering and industrial technology..

Pioneering Innovations

By the late 19th century, Voith revolutionized paper manufacturing and hydropower with groundbreaking technologies. The company developed advanced refiners, turbines, and automation systems that significantly improved efficiency. These innovations positioned Voith as a key supplier for industrial solutions, allowing it to expand its reach beyond Germany and into global markets.

International Expansion

In the early 20th century, Voith expanded internationally, supplying cutting-edge machinery worldwide. Its commitment to innovation and quality helped it secure major projects in energy, transportation, and manufacturing. As industrialization grew, Voith adapted and evolved, cementing its reputation as a trusted global leader in engineering and technology solutions.
